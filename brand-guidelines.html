02
Wordmark

The name set in Fraunces, single weight throughout, closed with an ember full stop. No icon or monogram leads; the type does the identity work.

Make It Land.
Make It Land.
Do
·Set every letter at a single weight, always, at any size.
·Keep the ember full stop, everywhere the wordmark appears.
·Give clear space equal to the cap-height of the M on all sides.
·Below 96px wide (22mm print), switch to the stacked lockup.
Don’t
·Never set “Land” heavier than the rest of the name.
·Never place the wordmark on photography. Palette backgrounds only.
·Don’t lead with the “M.” monogram; it stays on hold, wordmark first.
·No icons, shadows, gradients, or rounded corners on the mark.

04
Typography

Two families, two jobs. Fraunces carries display and headlines; Hanken Grotesk carries body, UI, and tracked micro-labels. Title or sentence case, never all-caps at display size.

Display · Fraunces
Judgment, set in type
Web display: opsz 90, wght 500. Print & doc packets: opsz 144, wght 600. Wordmark sets at 600. Tracking −1.5%.
Text & UI · Hanken Grotesk
Body copy, buttons, and navigation stay quiet so the serif and the figures carry the page.
Weight 400 to 500. Micro-labels 12px and under: tracked uppercase, Hanken only, never Fraunces. Tracking 0.14 to 0.22em.

05
Voice & tone

Plainspoken, evidence-led, calm authority. The metric does the boasting. No em-dashes, anywhere.

We sound like
·Plainspoken: short declaratives, active verbs.
·Evidence-led: the metric does the boasting.
·Client-centered: the client is the subject, not us.
We never sound like
·Hype or superlatives with no number behind them.
·Corporate filler, jargon, or emoji.
·Em-dashes, anywhere, including code.
Before · vague
“We deliver world-class branding that transforms your business.”
After · on-brand
Grew Adobe Fonts to 12M+ monthly users, and proved it drove revenue, retention, and growth.
